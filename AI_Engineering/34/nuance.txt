Nuance is all about subtlety and fine distinctions. It’s the small shades of meaning, tone, or detail that make something richer and more complex than it looks at first glance.  

For example:  
- In **language**, nuance might be the difference between “I’m fine” said warmly versus said curtly—the words are the same, but the tone changes the meaning.  
- In **art or design**, nuance could be the slight shift in color or texture that changes the mood of a painting.  
- In **debate or ideas**, nuance is recognizing that an issue isn’t black-and-white, but has layers, exceptions, and context that matter.  

Think of nuance like the difference between hearing a single note versus hearing a whole chord: the chord has depth because of the subtle interplay of notes.
