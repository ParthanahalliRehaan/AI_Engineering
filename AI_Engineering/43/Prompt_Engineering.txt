Got it — let me break down what your professor was saying in simpler terms. The whole lecture is about **prompt engineering**, which is basically the skill of writing good instructions for AI so it gives you the answer you actually want. Think of it like learning how to “talk” to the AI in its language.

---

### 🔑 Key Ideas from the Lecture
1. **Prompt engineering is an art, not just science**  
   - It’s about *how* you phrase things.  
   - The same question, asked differently, can give very different results.

2. **Be specific**  
   - Unlike search engines (Google), where you strip down to keywords, AI works better when you give *more detail*.  
   - Example:  
     - Bad: “How do I center something?”  
     - Better: “How do I center an element on a web page?”  
     - Best: “How do I center a div element horizontally and vertically using CSS Flexbox?”

3. **Use technical terms**  
   - If you know the right words (like *Flexbox*, *CSS*, *div*), include them.  
   - This helps the AI narrow down exactly what you mean.

4. **Provide context/examples**  
   - If you paste your HTML or CSS, the AI can tailor its answer to your actual code.  
   - The more context you give, the closer the answer will be to something you can copy-paste directly.

5. **Iterate on your prompt**  
   - You might not get the perfect answer the first time.  
   - Adjust your wording, add details, or clarify until the AI gives you what you need.

---

### 🖼 Example Walkthrough (from your professor)
- **Prompt 1:** “How do I center something?”  
  → AI gives a vague answer, covering text, images, Photoshop, etc. Too broad.

- **Prompt 2:** “How do I center an element on a web page?”  
  → Better. AI now focuses on web development, gives CSS examples.

- **Prompt 3:** “How do I center a div element horizontally and vertically using CSS Flexbox?”  
  → Perfect. AI gives a focused answer with HTML + CSS code you can use directly.

- **Prompt 4 (even better):** “Here’s my HTML. How do I center this div element horizontally and vertically using Flexbox, using the classes shown in my code?”  
  → AI tailors the solution exactly to your setup.

---

### 🎯 Main Takeaway
- With Google, you learned to *simplify* queries.  
- With AI, you need to *expand* queries: give details, context, and technical terms.  
- Think of it like teaching a junior developer — the clearer and more specific your instructions, the better the output.
