A **vector database** is like a search engine that searches by **meaning**, not exact words. [pinecone](https://www.pinecone.io/learn/vector-database/)
## Easy example
If you search:  
- “cheap phone with good camera”

A normal database looks for exact words.  
A vector database tries to understand the **idea** of your query and finds content with similar meaning, like:  
- “budget smartphone with high-quality photography.” [pinecone](https://www.pinecone.io/learn/vector-database/)
## How it works
- Text is turned into numbers called **embeddings**. [pinecone](https://www.pinecone.io/learn/vector-database/)
- Similar ideas get similar number patterns. [weaviate](https://weaviate.io/blog/what-is-a-vector-database)
- The database compares your query embedding with stored embeddings and returns the closest matches. [learn.microsoft](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/data-engineering/playbook/solutions/vector-database/)
## Why people use it
- It helps answer questions from **your own documents**. [pinecone](https://www.pinecone.io/learn/vector-database/)
- It can reduce hallucinations because the AI uses retrieved content instead of guessing. [milvus](https://milvus.io/blog/what-is-a-vector-database.md)
- It is useful for search, chat history, recommendations, and RAG systems. [weaviate](https://weaviate.io/blog/what-is-a-vector-database)
## Tiny analogy
- Normal database: “Find this exact word.”
- Vector database: “Find things that mean the same thing.”
