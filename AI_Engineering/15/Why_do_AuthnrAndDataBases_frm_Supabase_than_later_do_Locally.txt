Great question — you’re basically asking why developers often start with **Supabase (remote auth + database)** and only later move to **local setups**. Let’s break it down:

### 🌐 Why start with Supabase (remote)
- **Instant infrastructure**: Supabase gives you authentication, database, and APIs out of the box. No need to configure Postgres, JWT, or role-based access manually.
- **Rapid prototyping**: You can focus on building features instead of spending days setting up local Postgres, migrations, and auth flows.
- **Scalability baked in**: Since Supabase runs in the cloud, it’s already production-ready. You don’t have to worry about scaling your local machine.
- **Collaboration**: Remote DB/auth means your teammates can access the same environment without syncing local setups.

### 💻 Why move to local later
- **Performance & control**: Local inference or local DB queries avoid network latency and give you full control over the stack.
- **Cost management**: Running models or DB locally reduces dependency on API calls or hosted services, saving money long-term.
- **Experimentation**: You can tweak configs, run benchmarks, and test edge cases without worrying about breaking the shared cloud instance.
- **Offline capability**: Local setups let you keep working even without internet access.

### ⚖️ The workflow logic
1. **Prototype fast → Supabase**  
   Use Supabase for auth and DB while validating your idea. It’s quick, secure, and collaborative.
2. **Optimize later → Local**  
   Once the workflow stabilizes, migrate parts (like inference or DB queries) locally for speed, reproducibility, and cost efficiency.

Think of it like renting a fully furnished apartment (Supabase) while you figure out your lifestyle, then later building your own house (local setup) once you know exactly what you need.
