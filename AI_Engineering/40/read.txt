## 🎬 Initial Example

## 🚧 Limitation: No Memory
- Multi-turn conversations fail:
  - `"Is that film recommended for children?"` → AI asks for the title again.
  - `"Hi, my name is Gil"` → AI greets Gil.
  - `"What is my name?"` → AI doesn’t remember.

---

## 💡 Solution: Conversation History
- OpenAI’s **chat format** uses a `messages` array:
  - Each message has a `role` (`system`, `user`, `assistant`) and `content`.
- To add memory:
  - Append each AI response to the `chatMessages` array.
  - Supply the full conversation history in each request.

---

## 🔍 Implementation Details
- The AI’s reply is found in the `choices` array.
- That reply object (role + content) is pushed into `chatMessages`.

---

## 📜 System Prompt Updates
Two key instructions were added:

1. **Refer to Conversation History**  
   - If the answer isn’t in the immediate context, the AI should look at the **conversation history**.  
   - This ensures memory across turns (e.g., remembering the user’s name or preferences).

2. **Fallback Behavior**  
   - If unsure, reply: `"Sorry, I don’t know the answer."`  
   - Do not invent answers.  
   - Always speak in a **friendly, conversational tone** (like chatting with a friend).

---

   - AI recommends *Expendables*, *Top Gun Maverick*, *Blue Beetle*.

   - AI: `"Yes, your name is Gil."`
