## 🐕 Example Breakdown
You gave three phrases:
1. *Dogs are loyal companions*  
2. *Books contain a world of knowledge*  
3. *Canines are faithful friends*  

- **Lexical search** would look at the literal words. It would see “dogs” ≠ “canines” and might not group (1) and (3) together.  
- **Semantic search**, however, uses embeddings to capture meaning. It recognizes that “dogs” and “canines” are synonyms, and “loyal companions” ≈ “faithful friends.” So (1) and (3) cluster together, while (2) is clearly about something else.  

This is exactly what embeddings are designed to do: represent text in a way that encodes **meaning, context, and relationships**, not just surface-level words.

---

## 🧩 Two Core Parts of Semantic Search
The video you referenced ends with:  
> “Semantic search relies on two parts: creating vector embeddings from words, and something called vector similarity search.”

Let’s expand those:

### 1. **Vector Embeddings**
- **What they are**: Numerical representations of text (words, sentences, documents) in a high-dimensional space.  
- **How they’re created**: Models like BERT or OpenAI’s embedding models take text and output a vector (e.g., 768 dimensions).  
- **Why they matter**: Similar meanings → similar vectors. For example:
  - “dog” → [0.12, -0.45, …]  
  - “canine” → [0.11, -0.44, …]  
  These vectors end up close together in the embedding space.

### 2. **Vector Similarity Search**
- **What it does**: Finds the closest vectors to a query vector.  
- **How it works**: Uses mathematical measures like **cosine similarity** or **Euclidean distance** to compare vectors.  
- **Example**:  
  Query: *“relationship between humans and their pups”* → embedding vector.  
  Database: embeddings of phrases (1), (2), (3).  
  The query vector will be closest to (1) and (3), so those are returned as relevant results.  

This is the “search” part: instead of matching keywords, we’re matching **vector proximity**.

---

## ⚖️ Lexical vs Semantic Search in Practice
- **Lexical search**:  
  - Query: *“cats hate baths”*  
  - Returns only documents containing those exact words.  
- **Semantic search**:  
  - Query embedding: *“cats dislike water”*  
  - Finds documents with similar meaning, even if they don’t share words.  
  - Example: Spotify podcast search — “cats hate baths” matches an episode about “felines’ aversion to water,” even though the words differ.

---

## 🚀 Why This Matters
- **Lexical search** is like looking up a word in a dictionary.  
- **Semantic search** is like asking a friend who understands context and synonyms.  
- Modern systems often use **hybrid search**: first filter with keywords (fast), then rank results with semantic similarity (smart).
